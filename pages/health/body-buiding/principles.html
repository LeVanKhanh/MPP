# Training Principles ## Progressive Overload: Gradually increase resistance (weight, reps, sets, frequency) over time to force adaptation and growth. ## Specificity: Train muscles in ways that mimic desired movements (e.g., heavy lifting for strength, higher reps for pump). ## Mechanical Tension: Lift heavy (80-90% 1RM) for fewer reps (3-5) to create tension ## Metabolic Stress: Achieve "the pump" with higher reps (10-15+) and shorter rest periods. ## Muscle Damage: Incorporate slow negatives, full range of motion, and exercise variation to create micro-tears. ## Proper Technique & Form: Essential for targeting the right muscles and preventing injury. ## Consistency: Regular workouts are key; don't skip sessions or meals. # Nutritional Principles ## Protein: Provides amino acids, the building blocks for muscle repair and growth (fish, chicken, dairy, beans) ## Calories: Eat in a slight surplus (250-500 calories above maintenance) for growth, focusing on whole foods. ## Macronutrients: Balance protein, carbs (for fuel), and fats (for hormones). # Recovery Principles ## Rest Days: Muscles grow when resting, not during workouts. ## Sleep: Crucial for hormone regulation and muscle repair. ## Adequate Recovery: Allow enough time between training the same muscle group (e.g., 48 hours). # Key Takeaways - Training provides the stimulus, nutrition provides the building blocks, and rest allows the growth. - Focus on compound exercises (squats, deadlifts, presses) for overall mass, using isolation moves for refinement. - Listen to your body and adjust intensity/volume to avoid overtraining and injury.
