Introduction

Scrum, Agile and More

Published on: 28/03/2026

Scrum is a highly effective framework for organizing the software development process. However, when Scrum is applied in a purely mechanical way, it is not enough to guarantee the success of a product, a team, or the growth of people throughout that journey.

Scrum itself is not designed to solve every problem. It barely addresses capability management, performance management, decision-making approaches, or ways of working with people in complex situations. These gaps are not flaws, but intentional design choices in Scrum: they leave room for each organization to shape an operating model that fits its own context.

Scrum is not a complete answer. It is a framework that helps expose an organization's core issues. And those issues, rather than Scrum itself, are what determine whether a team truly moves forward.

In this topic, I will walk through each scope in Scrum theory to clarify recurring real-world problems and propose more practical ways to approach them.
