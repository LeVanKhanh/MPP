AI Terminologies

Quick reference for core AI and LLM concepts

Published: March 7, 2026

Foundation Terms

Core concepts that appear frequently in modern AI discussions and implementations.

LLM

Large Language Model is a neural network trained on large-scale text data to understand and generate human-like language for tasks such as summarization, question answering, and code generation.

NLP

Natural Language Processing is the field of AI focused on helping computers process, understand, and generate human language.

Prompt Engineering

The practice of designing and refining prompts so language models produce reliable, accurate, and context-appropriate outputs.

Application Frameworks and Patterns

Frameworks and architectures commonly used to build real-world AI products.

LangChain

A framework for building applications powered by language models, with tooling for prompt templates, chains, tool calling, memory, and integrations.

RAG

Retrieval-Augmented Generation is an architecture that combines information retrieval with generation, allowing a model to ground answers in external knowledge sources.

AI Automation Workflow

An orchestrated flow where AI models, APIs, and business logic are connected to automate repetitive tasks, decision support, or content generation processes.

Agent Concepts

Terms related to AI systems that can plan, decide, and execute multi-step tasks.

Agents

Software entities that use AI models to reason about goals, choose actions, and interact with tools or environments to complete tasks.

Agentic

Describes a behavior pattern where an AI system proactively plans and executes actions toward objectives rather than only producing one-off responses.

Autonomous Agents

Agents that can operate with limited human intervention, continuously making decisions and adapting actions based on feedback or changing context.

Summary

  • LLM, NLP, and Prompt Engineering form the conceptual foundation.
  • LangChain, RAG, and AI automation workflows support practical solution building.
  • Agents, agentic behavior, and autonomous agents represent the move toward action-oriented AI systems.
