Leadership Checklist

Essential steps for new leaders to succeed in their roles

1. Get Yourself a Notebook, Ask Questions and Listen

Begin by capturing observations and questions. Listen actively to understand before acting.

2. Study the Purpose and Goals of Your Organization

  • Why does your organization exist?
  • What is the main purpose of your organization?
  • What is the vision of your organization?
  • What is the plan for getting to that vision?
  • What is the organization’s message to the outside world?
  • What are your organization’s values?

3. Meet with Your Manager, Boss or Nearest Supervisor

  • How are you expected to contribute to those goals?
  • How are you expected to report and check on your progress toward the goals and their achievement?

4. Meet with the Whole Team

  • Introduce yourself as their new manager.
  • Share your initial focus (for example, items on this checklist).
  • Explain you will hold individual talks with everyone to get to know them.

6. Meet Individually with Each Team Member

  • What do you like about your job?
  • What are the biggest challenges you face?
  • What does your current workload look like?
  • What are your ambitions for your future work-wise?
  • What ideas do you have for improving the organization?

7. Address the Shift in Relationship

Discuss changes in roles and expectations to keep trust and clarity.

8. Do Operations Planning with Your Team

  • Break down the goals.
  • List activities.
  • Delegate: Who in the team should do which activity?
  • When should each activity be completed?
  • What is the expected outcome of each activity?
  • How will you measure and evaluate progress?
  • Confirm activities contribute to the overall vision and goal.

9. Coordinate with the Rest of the Organization

Align plans and dependencies with other teams and stakeholders.

10. Get Yourself a Mentor

Seek guidance from someone experienced who can challenge and support you.

11. Study to Get Smarter

Invest in learning to improve judgment, skills, and perspective.

12. Be a Role Model

Demonstrate the behaviors and standards you expect from the team.

13. Interact with Your Supervisor/Manager

Keep communication frequent and transparent to ensure alignment.

14. Change Your Focus

Adopt a team-oriented focus: if your team fails, you fail. Share credit with your team when you succeed to keep motivation strong.

15. Avoid Excessive Micromanaging or Abandonment

Provide the right level of support and autonomy—neither over-control nor neglect.

16. Resist the Temptation

Stay disciplined against distractions that pull you from priorities and leadership responsibilities.
