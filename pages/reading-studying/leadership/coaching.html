Tips for Effective Coaching

  • Ask open questions: Invite fuller answers and new perspectives.
  • Favor how-questions over why-questions: "How" keeps the focus on creative, forward steps; "why" often drifts into blame or post-mortems.
  • Set your own thoughts aside: Stay neutral and explore the other personâ€™s view of the situation.
  • Be genuinely interested: Curiosity builds trust and encourages candid sharing.
  • Align your intention: Be clear you are there to help them think, not to win or direct.
  • Paraphrase: Reflect back what you hear to confirm understanding and surface nuances.
  • Offer suggestions only if they are stuck: Coach the thinking process first; contribute ideas sparingly.
  • Help them find their own suggestions: Prompt them to generate options and choose their path.
  • Notice body language (yours and theirs): Posture, tone, and pace often reveal more than words.
  • Set timeframes: Bound the conversation and agree on timelines for actions or follow-ups.
