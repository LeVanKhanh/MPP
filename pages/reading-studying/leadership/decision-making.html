Decision Making

Systematic approaches and frameworks for effective decision making

Published: December 17, 2025

The Eight Steps of Decision Making

  1. Step 1: Identify problem: Define the issue clearly and specifically.
  2. Step 2: Clarify context: Understand constraints, stakeholders, and success criteria.
  3. Step 3: Brainstorm alternatives: Generate diverse options without judging them yet.
  4. Step 4: Analyze alternatives: Weigh pros, cons, risks, and feasibility for each option.
  5. Step 5: Choose alternative: Select the option that best fits goals and constraints.
  6. Step 6: Check ethics: Ensure the choice aligns with values, fairness, and integrity.
  7. Step 7: Implement decision: Execute with a clear plan, owners, and milestones.
  8. Step 8: Follow up, learn: Review outcomes, capture lessons, and adjust as needed.

Which Domain Are You Making Your Decision In?

  • Obvious: Stable context; cause-and-effect is clear to everyone.
  • Complicated: Multiple right answers; cause-and-effect exists but requires expertise to see.
  • Complex: Patterns emerge only after action; no clear right answer upfront.
  • Chaotic: No discernible order; act to establish stability before deciding.

Six Biases That Can Derail Your Decision

  • Confirmation bias: Seeking and remembering information that confirms existing beliefs.
  • Anchoring bias: Over-relying on the first piece of information or initial impression.
  • Overconfidence bias: Putting too much faith in your own knowledge, experience, or role.
  • Availability bias: Overestimating likelihood based on vivid, recent, or unusual memories.
  • Bandwagon bias: Adopting opinions or actions mainly because others have done so.
