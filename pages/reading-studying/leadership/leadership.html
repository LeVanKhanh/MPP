Essential concepts and practices for effective leadership and management

The Difference Between Management and Leadership

Management

  • Position: Appointment from above in the organization.
  • Organizing things: Operates in the "things" dimension; a defined role.
  • Formal: Authority is formal and role-based.

Leadership

  • Relationship: A vote of confidence from other people.
  • Relating, influencing: Operates in the relationships dimension.
  • Informal: Influence can be informal and earned.

The Four Levels of Communication

  1. Nothing: Surface talk (e.g., weather); shallowest level.
  2. Something: Talking about topics or events (sports, org chart, meetings, systems); still light depth.
  3. Somebody: Talking about someone not present (boss, coworker, family); more direct and personal.
  4. You and I: Deepest level; direct exchange between us (e.g., feedback) to develop trust and relationship.

Having a Clear Outcome

  • Something easily gets missed: Entering conversations without a desired outcome creates drift.
  • To be an effective communicator: Make the desired outcome crystal clear to sharpen your communication.

The Arrow of Credibility

Building credibility follows a systematic process from sensation to effect:

  1. Step 1: Sensation: What you experience with your senses.
  2. Step 2: Awareness: Noticing and acknowledging the sensation.
  3. Step 3: Insight: Making meaning (e.g., "I have too much to do at work").
  4. Step 4: Action: Acting based on the insight.
  5. Step 5: Effect: Owning the effects of your actions to build trust and credibility.

Conflict Management

Incompatible Strategies

Recognize when goals or approaches clash to choose an appropriate response.

The Five Conflict Styles

  • Fight: Prioritize your needs first.
  • Adapt: Prioritize the other person's needs, set your own aside.
  • Avoid: Engage neither your needs nor theirs; step away.
  • Compromise: Each side gets something, but not all they want.
  • Cooperate: Seek to meet both your needs and theirs.

Motivation

Communicating the Big Picture

"I'm building a cathedral" — connect daily work to a meaningful vision.

Clarify What Is Lacking

Diagnose gaps in skill and will to tailor support and challenge.

Leadership Dilemmas

Leaders must navigate these ongoing tensions and trade-offs:

  • Mission: Change or maintain.
  • Goal setting: Open for new options or stay the course.
  • Relations: Be close or keep distance.
  • Interdependence: Ask for help or teach others.
  • Decisions: Decide yourself or delegate.
  • Pace of change: Fast or slow.
  • Confidence: Fire team members or develop team members.
  • Loyalty: Support your team or serve upwards.

Four Parts of Management

  • Managing relationships.
  • Relating to a framework.
  • Delivering results.
  • Leading operations in a direction.

Responsibilities as a Manager

Maintains staff by recruiting, selecting, orienting, and training employees. Ensures a safe, secure, legal work environment. Develops growth opportunities. Accomplishes results by communicating expectations; planning, monitoring, and appraising results.

Plan, Organize, Staff, Lead, Control

  • Hire for skill and behavior gaps.
  • Lead and motivate the team.
  • Set and communicate team objectives and goals.
  • Set individual goals aligned to team goals.
  • Plan and organize workflows.
  • Monitor progress to ensure goals are met.
  • Develop and coach team members.
  • Keep employee experience high and turnover low.
