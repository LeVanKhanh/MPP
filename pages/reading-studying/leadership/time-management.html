SMART Goals

  • Specific: Clearly defined and focused.
  • Measurable: Quantifiable and trackable.
  • Achievable: Realistic and attainable.
  • Relevant: Aligned with your broader objectives and values.
  • Time-bound: Set within a specific timeframe.

OKRs

Objectives: Clear, qualitative descriptions of what you want to achieve.

Key Results: Quantitative measures that indicate progress toward your objectives.

Practical Tips

  1. Limit the number of objectives and key results to maintain focus.
  2. Involve your team in the OKR-setting process to foster buy-in and collaboration.
  3. Set both short-term and long-term OKRs to address immediate needs and strategic objectives.
  4. Regularly review and update your OKRs to reflect changing priorities and circumstances.
  5. Encourage open communication and feedback around OKRs within your team.
