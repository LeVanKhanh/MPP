Marxist Philosophy (Marx-Engels)

Fundamental principles of dialectical materialism

Updated: December 23, 2025

Materialist Premise

  • Matter precedes consciousness: Matter is the objective basis that exists independently of human consciousness
  • Matter determines consciousness: Consciousness is a product reflecting matter, the subjective image of the objective world

Dialectical Materialism

Dialectical materialism is the scientific methodology about the movement and development of things and phenomena in nature, society, and thought.

2 Fundamental Principles

Principle of Universal Connection

All things and phenomena always exist in interconnection, mutual interaction with each other. No thing or phenomenon exists independently or in isolation from other things and phenomena.

Principle of Development

Development is upward movement from low to high, from simple to complex, from less perfect to more perfect. Development is the inevitable trend of all things and phenomena.

3 Fundamental Laws

Law of Quantity - Quality

Gradual quantitative changes reaching a certain limit (nodal point) will lead to sudden qualitative changes (leap).

  • Quantity: The specification in terms of number, scale, and degree of things
  • Quality: The fundamental specification that makes a thing what it is and not another thing
  • Nodal point: The limit where quantitative change leads to qualitative change
  • Leap: The fundamental, sudden change in the quality of things

Law of Contradiction

The source of development is internal contradiction within things. Contradiction is the unity and struggle between opposing aspects within each thing and phenomenon.

  • Internal contradiction: The main driving force of development
  • External contradiction: The condition affecting development
  • Struggle and unity: The two sides of contradiction are both opposed and unified

Law of Negation of Negation

Development proceeds in a spiral path, negating the old while inheriting and developing at a higher level.

Example: Sprout negates seed → Mature plant negates sprout → Fruit negates plant (but the fruit contains new seeds at a higher level)

  • Dialectical negation: Both affirming and denying; both inheriting and developing
  • Inheritance: Retaining the positive elements of the old stage
  • Development: Bringing things to a higher level

6 Pairs of Fundamental Categories

1. The Particular and The Universal

  • The Particular: Concrete, individual things and phenomena
  • The Universal: Common points, similar essence of many things and phenomena
  • Relationship: The universal exists only in the particular and through the particular

2. Cause and Effect

  • Cause: The phenomenon that generates and affects other phenomena
  • Effect: The phenomenon that is generated and affected
  • Relationship: Cause generates effect, but effect can react back on cause

3. Essence and Phenomenon

  • Essence: The internal, profound, relatively stable aspect of things
  • Phenomenon: The external manifestation, unstable, easily changeable
  • Relationship: Essence is always manifested through phenomena, phenomena are manifestations of essence

4. Content and Form

  • Content: The sum of elements constituting things and their development process
  • Form: The mode of existence and manifestation of content
  • Relationship: Content determines form, form has a reaction on content

5. Necessity and Chance

  • Necessity: What is determined by internal causes, inevitably occurs under certain conditions
  • Chance: What is affected by external causes, may or may not occur
  • Relationship: Necessity manifests through chance, chance is the form of manifestation and supplement to necessity

6. Possibility and Reality

  • Possibility: What doesn't exist yet but can exist in the future
  • Reality: What currently exists, what is present
  • Relationship: Possibility can become reality when conditions are sufficient; reality is the result of transformation from possibility

Summary

Marxist philosophy provides a scientific and comprehensive system of views about the world and methods of understanding and transforming the world.

  • Materialist premise: Matter determines consciousness
  • 2 principles: Universal connection and Development
  • 3 laws: Quantity-Quality, Contradiction, Negation of negation
  • 6 pairs of categories: Help understand deeply the relationships between aspects of things
