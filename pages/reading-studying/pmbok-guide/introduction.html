The Standard for Project Management

The Standard for Project Management identifies project management principles that guide the behaviors and actions of project professionals and stakeholders engaged with projects.

Key Terms and Concepts

  • Outcome: An end result or consequence of a process or project. Outcomes can include outputs and artifacts, but focus on the benefits and value the project aims to deliver.
  • Portfolio: Projects, programs, subsidiary portfolios, and operations managed as a group to achieve strategic objectives.
  • Product: A quantifiable artifact that can be an end item itself or a component item.
  • Program: Related projects, subsidiary programs, and program activities managed in a coordinated manner to obtain benefits not available from managing them individually.
  • Project: A temporary endeavor undertaken to create a unique product, service, or result. The temporary nature indicates a beginning and an end to the project work or a phase of the work. Projects can stand alone or be part of a program or portfolio.
  • Project Management: The application of knowledge, skills, tools, and techniques to project activities to meet requirements—guiding project work to deliver intended outcomes using predictive, hybrid, or adaptive approaches.
  • Project Manager: The person assigned by the performing organization to lead the project team in achieving the project objective—facilitating team delivery and managing processes to realize the outcomes.
  • Project Team: The group performing the project work. It may include roles beyond the core team (e.g., business representatives, suppliers) needed to deliver the outcomes.
  • System for Value Delivery: The organizational network of portfolios, programs, projects, and operations that delivers value and enables strategy execution.
  • Value: The net benefit realized by stakeholders. Value can be tangible or intangible and is defined by the stakeholder context and expectations.
