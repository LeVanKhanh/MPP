The Standard for Project Management

Core concepts and terminology for value-driven project delivery

Published: December 17, 2025

The Standard for Project Management identifies project management principles that guide the behaviors and actions of project professionals and stakeholders engaged with projects.

Key Terms and Concepts

  • Outcome: An end result or consequence of a process or project; focuses on benefits and value delivered.
  • Portfolio: Projects, programs, subsidiary portfolios, and operations managed as a group to achieve strategic objectives.
  • Product: A quantifiable artifact that can be an end item itself or a component item.
  • Program: Related projects, subsidiary programs, and program activities managed in a coordinated manner to obtain benefits not available from managing them individually.
  • Project: A temporary endeavor undertaken to create a unique product, service, or result; can stand alone or be part of a program or portfolio.
  • Project Management: Application of knowledge, skills, tools, and techniques to meet requirements using predictive, hybrid, or adaptive approaches.
  • Project Manager: Leads the project team to achieve the objective, facilitating delivery and managing processes to realize outcomes.
  • Project Team: Group performing the project work; may include roles beyond the core team as needed for outcomes.
  • System for Value Delivery: Organizational network of portfolios, programs, projects, and operations that delivers value and enables strategy execution.
  • Value: Net benefit realized by stakeholders, tangible or intangible, defined by context and expectations.
