Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs

A content theory of motivation focusing on human needs

Published: December 17, 2025

Overview

  • According to Maslow, we have five categories of needs: physiological, safety, love, esteem, and self-actualization.
  • In this theory, higher needs in the hierarchy begin to emerge when people feel they have sufficiently satisfied the previous need.

The Five Categories

  1. Physiological Needs: Basic survival needs such as food, water, shelter, and sleep.
  2. Safety Needs: Security, stability, and protection from harm.
  3. Love and Belonging: Social connections, relationships, and sense of belonging.
  4. Esteem Needs: Self-respect, achievement, recognition, and respect from others.
  5. Self-Actualization: Realizing personal potential, self-fulfillment, and personal growth.
