Herzberg's Two-Factor Theory

Understanding motivators and hygiene factors in the workplace

Published: December 17, 2025

Overview

Herzberg's Two-Factor Theory distinguishes between factors that cause job satisfaction (motivators) and factors that cause job dissatisfaction (hygiene factors). These two sets of factors operate independently.

Factors for Satisfaction (Motivators)

Factors for satisfaction are called motivators. These are intrinsic to the work itself and lead to positive satisfaction.

  • Achievement: Sense of accomplishment and success
  • Recognition: Acknowledgment of contributions and efforts
  • The work itself: Interesting, challenging, and meaningful tasks
  • Responsibility: Autonomy and ownership over work
  • Advancement: Opportunities for promotion and career growth
  • Growth: Personal and professional development

Factors for Dissatisfaction (Hygiene Factors)

Factors for dissatisfaction are called hygiene factors. These are extrinsic to the work and their absence causes dissatisfaction, but their presence does not necessarily motivate.

  • Company policies: Organizational rules and procedures
  • Supervision: Quality of management and leadership
  • Relationship with supervisor and peers: Interpersonal dynamics at work
  • Work conditions: Physical work environment and resources
  • Salary: Compensation and benefits
  • Status: Position and standing within the organization
  • Security: Job stability and safety

Key Insight

Improving hygiene factors prevents dissatisfaction but doesn't create satisfaction. True motivation comes from the motivators - the intrinsic aspects of work that provide meaning, growth, and achievement.
