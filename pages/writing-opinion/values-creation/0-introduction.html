Preface

I write these lines not because I believe I have understood life, but because I am deeply aware that what I have learned and experienced—if not written down—will fade with time and disappear as I grow old. Writing, for me, is a way to preserve the most important insights I have accumulated about creating value in life—for myself, for others, and for the society in which I live.

I am not sure how many people this book will help. For some, it may not be new; for others, it may not be deep enough. But I believe that, at the very least, it will be a guide written with seriousness and heart—something I can leave to my children and grandchildren in the future, as a foundation for them to understand that value does not arise on its own, and that life becomes meaningful when we create value for others.

What is presented here is not a formula for success, nor a promise of an easy life. These are foundational principles that I believe can apply to most people—especially those who are struggling, without a frame of reference or clear guidance to define the path they wish to take and the goals they wish to pursue in life.

This book is, in essence, a personal philosophy framework—a personal system of thinking about what value is, why we must create it, and how to gradually build a meaningful life through action, discipline, capability, and sustained contribution to others. If these pages help anyone see their path a little more clearly, then for me, the value of writing them has been created.
