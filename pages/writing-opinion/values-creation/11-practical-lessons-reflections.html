11 - Practical Lessons and Reflections

An open chapter to observe, compare, and find your own formula

Published: February 2, 2026

I once intended to write this chapter with real-life images of people who had succeeded. I thought that if we placed their lives alongside what was presented in previous chapters, we would see a fairly clear “formula”: they did this, therefore they achieved that.

But the more I thought, the more I felt I needed to stop.

Because what I just described can easily be personal bias: I see the world through my own frame of reference, then unconsciously fit real lives into the pattern I have already built. I might be “reading” other people’s success through my formula — not through their full reality.

Therefore, I choose not to write this chapter as a list of illustrations. I leave it open — truly open — so that you, my dear reader, can observe and test for yourself: around you, are there people who have done similar things? How much of what is discussed here did they apply? And are they truly successful?

Or perhaps you will see other paths: people who succeed through a completely different formula — even the opposite. If you see that, it is entirely wonderful. Because then you are no longer seeking confirmation for a pre-made pattern, but beginning to form your own path.

In the end, the path of value is a personal one. Each person chooses a different way and method. Everything here should only be considered as reference.

And knowledge or skill, in the end, does not lie in how much we hear from others — but in how we make it our own, or reach it through our own experience and choices.
